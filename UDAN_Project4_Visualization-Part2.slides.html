GoBike Share Usage Pattern Analysis¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, I will to look at the bike ride usage pattern in SanFrancisco Bay Area in Feb, 2019. The main focus was on biking duration on different time and user groups.

Dataset Overview¶

This data set includes information about individual rides made in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area in Feb,2019. The data consisted of approximately 170K rows with attributes of user's profile (age,gender,subscription status, Bike Share all program enrollment status etc) and bike ride behavior (start/end station, bike ride duration,start/end time etc).

Bike Ride Duration¶

Next, I have investigated duration of bike trips. When I have plotted the histogram, I found that most of data lies under below 50 minutes(1 hr). The distribution is heavily skewed to the right showing that most bikes are generally used for short trips.

Distance of Bike Trips¶

Even if the start and end position cannot represent absolute travel distances, I have tried to plot histogram to see how far apart between the start and end bike stand. It seems that distance between start and end bike stations are under few kilimeters(~3 km) range. Distance of 0 or distance under few hundreds meters(~<200 meter) may represent round trips.

When I have clustered rider group into two by their ages 1) Older than 40 yrs old and 2) less than equal 40 yrs and plotted bike ride duration vs. hour on every days of week, I have found that older group rides longer than younger group.
